General Questions

What is a vehicle history report?

A vehicle history report is a comprehensive document that provides detailed information about a vehicle's past. This includes accident history, title information, ownership records, service history, mileage records, and more. Our reports help you make an informed decision when buying or selling a used vehicle.

Why should I get a vehicle history report?

A vehicle history report can reveal hidden problems that aren't visible during a visual inspection. It helps you:

  • Avoid vehicles with serious accident damage
  • Identify odometer fraud
  • Check for title issues like salvage or flood damage
  • Verify service and maintenance records
  • Confirm the number of previous owners
How accurate are your reports?

Our reports are compiled from over 50 reliable data sources including government agencies, insurance companies, auto auctions, and service centers. While we strive for 100% accuracy, we cannot guarantee that all information is complete as some incidents may go unreported. We recommend using our report in conjunction with a professional vehicle inspection.

Report Content

What information is included in a CarVerifi report?

Our comprehensive reports include:

  • Accident history and damage reports
  • Title information (clean, salvage, rebuilt, etc.)
  • Ownership history and number of previous owners
  • Service and maintenance records
  • Odometer readings and potential rollback alerts
  • Recall information
  • Lemon history
  • Vehicle specifications
  • Theft records
  • Flood damage history
How far back does your data go?

Our data sources vary, but we typically have records going back to the early 1990s for most vehicles. For newer vehicles, we often have complete histories from the date of first registration. The availability of specific records depends on when various reporting systems were implemented and the reporting practices in different states.

Do you provide information about service records?

Yes, when available, we include service and maintenance records in our reports. These records come from various sources including dealerships, repair shops, and service centers that report to our data partners. However, not all service providers report their records, so there may be gaps in the service history.

Technical Questions

What is a VIN and where can I find it?

A VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) is a unique 17-character code assigned to every vehicle. You can typically find it:

  • On the dashboard near the windshield (driver's side)
  • On the driver's side door jamb
  • On vehicle registration documents
  • On insurance cards
  • On the vehicle title
How long does it take to generate a report?

Our reports are generated instantly in most cases. After you provide the VIN and complete your payment, the report is typically available within seconds. In rare cases where we need to gather additional information from multiple sources, it might take up to 24 hours. You'll receive an email notification when your report is ready.

Can I access my reports later?

Yes, all purchased reports are saved to your account and can be accessed at any time. You can view, download, or print them as needed. We recommend saving important reports as vehicle histories can be updated with new information over time.
